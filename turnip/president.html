Who am I?
I’m Lavender, a dog lover, a food lover, and a 3rd year (soon to be 4th year) Software Engineering student. Ever since first year, I’ve been involved with CSESoc in some form or another. Without this society, and the amazing teams in the past that have lead its success, I definitely wouldn’t be the person that I am today. Now, it’s time to give back.

... & Other Societies

With all of this involvement in clubs outside of CSESoc, I've got a first hand (and fresh) perspective on all the different ways that societies are run. 2016 CSESoc Goals


Sponsorship
I want to ensure that our members can get the most out of our strong relationship with our sponsors. I want to make sure that sponsors have the opportunity to come onto campus, allowing for interaction on a more personal level. By taking the society in this direction, this will create more meaningful and useful connections that will be useful for the career progression of our members.

CSE Revue
I hope to also improve relations with CSE Revue. Both societies are incredibly important in the personal development of CSE Students. A lot of teams (and members!) overlap between the two societies. I hope to have more joint events between both societies. It would not only provide valuable learning experiences for both organisers, but also gives a chance for people to meet one another in their respective societies.

Girls & Code
Increasing the involvement of women in computing is something that I feel passionate about. As president, I hope to leverage CompClub's* existing workshops, connections, and people to reach out to more girls in primary and high schools.
*CSESoc's High School Computing Team

CSESoc + UNSW
My experience in Arc has put me in contact with a lot of societies and people within the university. I hope to use this to increase CSESoc’s presence within the UNSW community. Whether it would be something small, like organising inter-society sports events in practice for Engsoc sports day or to utilising spaces like the Michael Crouch Innovation Centre (or the resources from it, at the very least) to further student creativity, especially in DevSpace.

Alumni++
I hope to increase the number of alumni talks and workshops in CSESoc. Since it’s CSESoc’s 10th birthday next year - It would be amazing to get even more alumni to share their experiences after graduation, as well as teach any important skills that they’ve picked up along the way.
